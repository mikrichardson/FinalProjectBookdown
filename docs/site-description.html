Chapter 3 Site Description

The Cache la Poudre River, commonly referred to as the Poudre River, makes up 126 miles of Colorado’s 107,000 miles of rivers. The Poudre River is Colorado’s only designated “Wild & Scenic” River. The Wild and Scenic Rivers Act is national legislation that protects over 13,400 miles of rivers and streams. The river originates in Rocky Mountain National Park, flowing east through Fort Collins and eventually converging with the South Platte River near Greeley. The river corridor experiences several diverse ecosystems ranging from high alpine mountains to rolling plains. The gauge site used for this analysis is located at the Lincoln Bridge in Fort Collins.

The Poudre River site at Lincoln Bridge is:

  • Downstream of only a little bit of urban stormwater

  • Near Odell Brewing CO

  • Near an open space area and the Poudre River Trail

  • Downstream of many agricultural diversions
